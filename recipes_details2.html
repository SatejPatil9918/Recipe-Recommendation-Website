Egg Munchurian

Egg Manchurian is a spicy, tangy, and satisfying dish perfect for lovers of bold flavors. Start by boiling 4 eggs, peeling them, and cutting them into halves. Prepare a batter by mixing ½ cup of cornflour, ¼ cup of all-purpose flour, salt, pepper, and a splash of water to form a thick paste. Dip the egg halves in the batter and deep-fry until golden and crispy. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté 1 chopped onion, 1 tablespoon of minced garlic, and 1 tablespoon of ginger until fragrant. Add 1 sliced green chili and ½ cup of chopped bell peppers, stir-frying until slightly soft. Stir in 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of red chili sauce, 1 tablespoon of tomato ketchup, and ¼ cup of water. Let it simmer until the sauce thickens. Toss in the fried eggs and coat them well with the sauce. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot with fried rice or noodles for a fiery, flavorful treat.

Recipe

Egg Manchurian is a bold, spicy, and flavorful Indo-Chinese dish that brings a delightful twist to any meal. To begin preparing this vibrant recipe, start by boiling four eggs, then peel and halve them once cooled. In a bowl, make a thick batter by mixing cornflour, all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt and pepper, and a splash of water. Dip each egg half into the batter and deep-fry until they are golden and crispy.

Once the vegetables are slightly softened and the aromatics are well sautéed, add two tablespoons of soy sauce, one tablespoon of red chili sauce, and one tablespoon of tomato ketchup to the pan, stirring everything together to create a rich, tangy, and spicy base. For added depth, you can mix in a splash of vinegar or a pinch of sugar to balance the flavors. Let the sauce simmer gently for a few minutes until it thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon. At this stage, carefully add the crispy fried egg halves to the sauce, gently folding them in so they remain intact while soaking up the bold, savory flavors. Toss in some chopped spring onions and a handful of bell peppers for crunch and color. Season the dish with salt and pepper to taste, ensuring a perfect balance of heat and umami.

Finally, gently toss in the fried eggs, coating them evenly with the sauce. Garnish with freshly chopped spring onions and serve hot with fried rice or noodles for a fiery, satisfying dish.Allow the Egg Manchurian to simmer for another minute, just until everything is heated through and well combined. Garnish with more spring onions or fresh coriander for a vibrant finish, and serve hot with steamed rice or noodles for a comforting, flavor-packed meal.
